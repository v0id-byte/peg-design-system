"We have the reserves." — Says who?
What most issuers provide
Attestation
Management says the reserves exist. An accountant confirms the numbers on paper — but does not independently trace each asset back to its real-world source.
The gold standard
Full Audit
An independent auditor traces every reserve asset to its real-world source and formally certifies those assets genuinely exist.
Stability rests on trust in the proof — not just the promise.
Tether's comprehensive independent audit remains pending as of 2025.

What the law actually requires.
GENIUS Act  ·  Pub. L. 119–27  ·  Signed July 18, 2025
① 1:1 Reserves — Cash or sub-93-day Treasuries only; no high-yield, no risky assets.
② No Interest to Holders — Stablecoins cannot pay yield or offer staking to users.
③ Jurisdictional Carve-out — Not a security, not a commodity; SEC & CFTC are excluded.
④ Dual-track Regulation — >$10B → Federal (Fed / OCC)  ·  <$10B → state-level option.
GENIUS Act, Pub. L. 119–27 (2025); Paul Hastings LLP (2025); DLA Piper (2025); Sidley Austin LLP (2025)

14
Three deeper risks.
① Systemic Risk
Fire Sales
A run on a major stablecoin forces rapid mass liquidation of Treasuries — spreading crypto stress into traditional money markets.
② Monetary Sovereignty
Seigniorage Loss
If private stablecoins displace cash, governments lose seigniorage income and central banks lose M1/M2 control.
③ Consumer Risk
Fraud Profitability
No law requires issuers to return stolen funds to victims. Frozen criminal assets can legally accrue to the issuer.
Brookings (2025); NY State AG James et al. (2025); Fidelity Investments (2025)
